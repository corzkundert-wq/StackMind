  • Primary offer: packaged, grant-eligible marketing + pre-order program
  • Target: builders with current or probable grant awards (highest ROI)
  • Tactical focus: speed (30/60/90 day impact), grant-payable billing, and partner channels

Problem: Financial & Time Frictions

Operators often have grant dollars but won’t spend internal cash; simultaneously they're under time pressure where subscriber velocity dictates valuation and options.
  • Available grant funds exist, but operators resist out-of-pocket spend
  • Slow subscriber growth compresses exit and funding options
  • Current offers fail to address both financial friction and urgency
Notes: Two simultaneous frictions: affordability (operators won't spend cash) and urgency (they must accelerate growth to avoid valuation decay). Any product must remove financial friction and deliver fast, measurable impact.
3

Strategic Opportunity: Grant-Payable Primary Offer

Make a grant-eligible, done-for-you sales & marketing package our core product to eliminate the primary buying objection and convert grant budgets into procurement of our services.
  • Design the offer as a line-item that fits common grant budgets (marketing, pre-orders, lead capture)
  • Explicitly state 'grant funds can cover this' in materials and quotes
  • Prioritize outreach to grant winners/applicants and grant advisors
Notes: By packaging our service to align with grant allowable expenses and making the billing/fulfillment flow explicit, we transform procurement from discretionary to straightforward. This is a lower-resistance acquisition channel compared to cash sales.
4

Product Design: Core Components & Deliverables

A compact, repeatable product that delivers measurable subscriber acceleration in 30/60/90 day increments and is easy to procure via grants.
  • Core: pre-order campaign + lead capture + targeted outreach + fulfillment flow
  • Deliverables: outreach templates, sales collateral, billing documentation for grants
  • Timeline guarantees: clear 30/60/90 day impact milestones
Notes: The product must be concrete and auditable for grant administrators: scope, costs, deliverables, and milestones. Guarantee specific outcomes in short windows to match operators' time sensitivity and increase conversion.
5

Go-to-Market: Target Segments & Prioritization

Focus resources on fiber-builders and operators with grant awards or high probability of awards — they produce the best pre-order convertibility and case-study potential.
  • Segment priority: fiber-builders with active or imminent grants
  • Tactical outreach tied to procurement timelines and build schedules
  • Include a pre-order playbook to convert grant wins into committed subscribers before build completion
Notes: Concentrate sales effort where grant dollars + build plans align. These clients can fund marketing, produce pre-orders, and generate scalable case studies. Tailor timing to their procurement windows to close faster.
6

Sales Messaging & Outreach Framework

Adopt messaging that removes identity-based resistance to outsourcing and highlights grant funding and rapid impact.
  • Reframe outsourcing as leverage: emphasize owners keep core work while we scale subscribers
  • Create outbound templates that explicitly note eligibility for grant payment and show billing flow
  • Use an urgency framework: diagnostics that flag 'running out of moves' and escalate offers
Notes: Owners are protective of their role — we must normalize outsourcing as a strategic lever. Templates should be direct: explain how the service maps to grant categories, the billing path, and immediate outcomes to trigger action under time pressure.
7

Partnerships & Channel Strategy

Accelerate procurement and reach by partnering with grant advisors, local economic development agencies, and procurement consultants.
  • Prioritize partnerships with grant advisors to get early access to winners/applicants
  • Co-develop procurement-ready collateral that advisors can include in grant spend recommendations
  • Use partner referrals to fast-track procurement checks and remove bureaucracy
Notes: Grant advisors are force multipliers: they validate eligibility and can recommend our product as a line-item. Build simple partner materials (SOW + billing templates) so advisors can confidently refer us into procurement processes.
8

Delivery & Client Experience

Operationalize rapid deployment with diagnostics, fast-deploy tactics, training modules, and documented fulfillment to maintain client control while we execute.
  • Fast-deploy tactics: lead stewardship, pre-order campaigns, templated outreach
  • Short training modules to ease ownership transfer and preserve client control
  • Documented billing/fulfillment flow for grant reporting and compliance
Notes: We must be able to deploy quickly and hand back control where appropriate. Short trainings reduce identity friction and ensure clients feel in charge. Every engagement must produce grant-reportable artifacts to satisfy auditors.
9

Metrics, Pricing & Expected ROI

Define clear KPIs, price as grant-eligible line items, and model conservative outcomes for the first pilot cohort.
  • KPIs: pre-orders committed, qualified leads, conversion rate, subscribers added in 30/60/90 days
  • Pricing: modular line-items aligned to grant expense categories (campaign + fulfillment + reporting)
  • Expected pilot outcomes: X pre-orders / Y qualified leads in 90 days (use conservative conversion assumptions)
Notes: We sell measurable outcomes. For a pilot, set conservative targets and report weekly. Pricing must be transparent and allocable to common grant categories. Track CAC, payback period, and incremental valuation impact where possible.
