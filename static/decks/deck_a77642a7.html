1

Executive Summary

Concise overview of the business proposition, market opportunity, traction to date, and the investment or decision requested.
  • Business: what we do and for whom
  • Opportunity size and growth rate
  • Key traction metrics to date
  • Ask: capital, partnership, or approval
Notes: I'll open with the core thesis: who we are, why the market is sizable now, what we've proven so far, and the concrete ask. Keep attention on return potential and near-term milestones.
2

Problem Definition

Define the specific problem experienced by target customers, quantify pain points, and show current solutions' shortcomings.
  • Clear articulation of customer pain (time, cost, risk)
  • Market segments most affected
  • Existing solutions: gaps and inefficiencies
  • Quantified impact on customer economics
Notes: State the problem in measurable terms—lost revenue, wasted labor hours, regulatory risk. I want the audience to feel urgency and understand why existing options fail.
3

Market Opportunity

Analytical sizing of the addressable market, target segments, and growth drivers that make this a timely investment.
  • Total Addressable Market (TAM), Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM)
  • Target segment and initial beachhead
  • Growth drivers: technology, regulation, behavior shifts
  • Key adoption assumptions and timelines
Notes: Present the numbers and the assumptions behind them. Be explicit about the beachhead market we will capture first and why it scales. I will highlight conservative and aggressive scenarios.
4

Our Solution

Describe the product/service, core capabilities, and how it solves the identified problem better than alternatives.
  • Core features and value propositions
  • Differentiation: speed, cost, quality, compliance
  • Customer outcomes and proof points
  • Implementation and integration footprint
Notes: Explain the solution in plain terms and link each capability to a customer benefit. I will use one or two short customer examples to validate effectiveness.
5

Business Model

Revenue model, pricing strategy, unit economics, and scalability levers that drive margin expansion over time.
  • Primary revenue streams (subscription, transaction, services)
  • Pricing rationale and competitive positioning
  • Unit economics: CAC, LTV, payback period
  • Operational levers to improve margins at scale
Notes: Highlight the path to profitability: current unit economics and levers to improve them. Be candid about customer acquisition costs and how we'll reduce them.
6

Go-to-Market Strategy

Targeted GTM plan: channels, partnerships, sales motions, and early traction tactics to accelerate adoption.
  • Primary channels: direct sales, channel partners, digital
  • Customer acquisition playbook and funnel metrics
  • Partnerships and ecosystem strategy
  • Key hires and investments for GTM execution
Notes: Outline the top three GTM priorities and expected timelines. I will commit to measurable KPIs and the resources required to hit them.
7

Financial Forecast

Three-year forward financials: revenue, gross margin, EBITDA trajectory, and underlying assumptions driving the model.
  • Projected revenue and margin profile by year
  • Capital requirements and burn rate
  • Break-even and cash runway scenarios
  • Key sensitivities and upside drivers
Notes: Present a concise forecast with the assumptions called out. I'll emphasize cash runway under base and downside cases and the funding needed to reach key value-creating milestones.
8

Competitive Landscape & Moat

Map of competitors, relative positioning, and the sustainable advantages that protect our economics.
  • Direct and indirect competitors overview
  • Our positioning on core attributes (price, features, service)
  • Sustainable moats: data, distribution, partnerships, IP
  • Potential competitive responses and our counters
Notes: Be explicit about who can attack our position and why we will hold advantage. I want to show defensibility and realistic threats with mitigation paths.
9

Risks & Mitigations

Top risks to execution, market, product, and finance with concrete mitigation strategies for each.
  • Execution risk and contingency plans
  • Market adoption risk and validation steps
  • Technical or regulatory risks and compliance roadmap
  • Financial risks and capital management approaches
Notes: Acknowledge material risks candidly and present concrete, prioritized mitigations. I will stress that risk management is baked into the plan, not an afterthought.
10

Ask & Next Steps

Specific request (funding amount or decision), use of proceeds, milestones tied to the ask, and immediate next steps if approved.
  • Exact ask: amount, type, and valuation expectations if applicable
  • Use of funds mapped to milestones and timelines
  • Desired decision timeline and governance terms
  • Immediate actions post-approval
Notes: Close with a precise ask and a pragmatic execution plan. I will specify milestones that trigger the next tranche or decision, and propose a timeline for commitment and first deliverables.
